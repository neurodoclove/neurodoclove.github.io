			<h2>Valleys</h2>
		</header>
		<p>The average amount of time it takes for a scientific discovery to reach clinical practice is <b> 17 years</b>. Why is this?  The reasons are complex, but even when a treatment shows promise, even when it succeeds it human trials, it has to cross what clinical trialists refer to as a' valley of death'. This particular valley is adoption of treatment by practitioners. </p>
		<p>You wouldn't be faulted for thinking that once a treatment has been proven to be effective, that adoption should be simple. Unfortunately, our systems of care are rarely designed to rapidly implement new interventions effectively. Even when they do adopt new interventions, it can still be incredibly difficult for patients to connect with the treatments they need because our healthcare systems are often exceptionally difficult to navigate.</p>
		<p>So, what do we do?  Do our best, make modest adjustments where we can, and continue operating within a broken system <b>or</b> commit to fundamentally changing the way that care is delivered and start designing a system that can meet the actual needs of both providers and patients.</p>
